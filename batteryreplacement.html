How to Replace a Car Battery

1. Locate your battery

Reference your car's manual for the location of your 12-volt battery. Most car batteries will be under the hood or below the trunk floor. If located anywhere else, please visit your local mechanic.

2. Obtain a Replacement Battery

Although most Auto Stores offer free installation when you purchase a battery from the store, the battery will cost significantly more. Purchasing a battery from your local Walmart, Costco, Sam's Club, and Batteries Plus will save you money.

3. Identify Terminals

A battery will a postive and negative connection. The postive connection is often identify with a red cover or + symbol near the connection. The negative is often a bear wire with no covering with a - symbol.

4. Remove Connections and Hold Downs

A battery contain hazordus materials and gloves should be worn for the remainder of this repair. Using a socket set or wrench set remove your postive and negative connection on your old battery. Look for any bracket at top, bottom, or sides of the battery securing it to your vehicle.

5. Remove old battery

Your old battery may or may not have a handle to lift it out of your vehicle, if not be sure that you have a good grip on the battery when removing it to pervent any damage if dropped.

6. Cleaning battery terminals

Using your terminal cleaner brush the terminls until you see bare metal for the best connection. Apply your white lithum grease to your connections and termianls to pervent corrosion.

7. Installing New Battery

Lift the new battery into your vehicle set in place of where your old battery was. Ensure your new battery is in the smae orentation as your old battery. Tighten down any hold downs secure your battery with your socket or wrench set.

7. Connect New Battery

Connect your new battery ensuring that you do not connect it backwards. If connected backwards severe damage to vehicle's electronics is possible. Using your socket set or wrench tighten down your connections.

You have succsefully Replaced Your Battery!
