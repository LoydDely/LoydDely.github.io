Replacing Fuse


Tools / Parts Needed

  • Fuse
  • Needle Nose Pliers

1. Locate your Fuse Box

Depending on the electrical issues in your vehicle, the desired fuse box may be located in your vehicle's interior, trunk, or engine bay. Reference your owner's manual for the exact location of your desired fuse box.

2. Removing Use

After locating and accessing your fuse box, use needle nose pliers to remove the desired fuse by pulling away from the fuse box.

3. Getting Replacment Fuse

Your fuse should have a number on the top to identify how many amps it is. Fuses move up in 5amp increments. Take your blown fuse to the auto parts store, compare it with the available fuse, and find one matching yours. Fuse are standard sizes; all auto parts stores carry your fuse.

4. Installing New Fuse

Push the new fuse into the space of the old fuse until it is flush with the other fuses around it. Check if the electrical issue is resolved; visit your nearest mechanic if the problem persists.

You have successfuly Replaced your Fuse
